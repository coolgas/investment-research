## 3. Why Now

Four catalysts converged in a single week:

**1. Samsung Record Profit as a Catalyst.** Samsung reported a 19-fold surge in Q2 profit on Jul 6. Investors sold into strength after a ~150% YTD rally. Bloomberg's "Samsung Results Trigger Stock Rotation to Less Loved Sectors" (Jul 7) and "Samsung's Record Profit Fails to Impress After AI Chip Rally" (Jul 6) both capture the dynamic: the news was good, but priced in.

**2. SK Hynix $28B IPO Uncertainty.** The NY listing -- largest-ever US IPO by a foreign company -- faces headwinds from extreme chip stock volatility. Until pricing is set, uncertainty overhang persists.

**3. Rotation Narrative Gains Steam.** Investors are rotating out of AI winners into unloved sectors (value, energy, financials). "Nuveen's Cooper Sees Rotation Back Into Hyperscalers" (Jul 7) captures the pivot.

**4. China Domestic Silicon Overhang.** A Jul 7 survey showed Chinese firms shifting away from NVDA toward domestic AI suppliers, adding structural demand uncertainty for the GPU ecosystem.

---

## 4. NVDA's Strange YTD

NVDA is up only <span class="up">+3.55%</span> YTD while SMH is up <span class="up">+61.88%</span>. This is the most important divergence in the semi landscape. The 2026 semi rally ran without the GPU king -- it was about the ecosystem (equipment, memory, analog, foundry), not NVDA directly. NVDA peaked earlier in the year and has been rangebound near $190-200. This means:

- There is no "NVDA bubble" to burst in this selloff.
- The selloff is about extended cyclical names taking profits, not an AI-thesis unwind.
- If the rotation reverses, NVDA has room to play catch-up.

## 7. Outlook

**Short-term (1-4 weeks):** Rotation pressure likely continues. SK Hynix IPO pricing is the next catalyst -- if it prices below expectations, it could signal peak cycle; if strong, it would re-validate semi demand. Samsung sell-the-news may take another week to wash out.

**Medium-term (1-3 months):** Semi fundamentals remain intact. AI capex is still accelerating, memory cycle is driven by HBM demand, equipment orders are strong. The selloff looks like profit-taking in a bull market, not the start of a bear cycle. YTD gains of <span class="up">+60-212%</span> across the sector were not built on air.

**Key levels to watch:**
- SMH support at <span class="num">580</span> (current: <span class="num">604.30</span>, ~4% downside from here)
- NVDA support at <span class="num">190</span> (current: <span class="num">195.55</span>, ~3% downside)
- Below <span class="num">580</span> on SMH would suggest the rotation is deeper than tactical

**SMGB.L note:** At <span class="down">-7.25%</span> on the week, the damage is proportionate to beta. Higher beta means amplified moves both ways. If semi sentiment recovers, SMGB.L should outperform on the upside given its <span class="up">+83.51%</span> YTD and leveraged exposure to the same names driving the selloff today.
